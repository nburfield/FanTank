The Story

Lincoln Perry, widely known as the controversial character "Stepin Fetchit," was Hollywood’s first black movie star during the 20’s and 30’s. His character was a distorted comedic image of what some white folks thought of African Americans at the time. What they didn’t know, and what most people don’t know today, is that he was a devout Catholic, and activist and writer for the Chicago Defender standing up against crooked booking agents and theaters owners, as well as fighting with the major studio heads for equal pay for black actors. Though his life of fame and fortune brought career challenges and personal tragedies, Lincoln starred in films with Hollywood’s elite, from Mae West and Will Rogers to name a few and hobnobbed with boxers Jack Johnson and Cassius Clay.

Creative Team

Antwone Fisher

Director

Antwone Fisher is an American screenwriter, Director, poet, lecturer, and bestselling author. Antwone is the writer, co-producer and subject of the Fox Searchlight Pictures classic film, ANTWONE FISHER. His autobiographical book Finding Fish: A memoir is a New York Times and national bestseller.

Antwone has worked in Hollywood for 25 years as a screenwriter and producer, with an impressive fifteen writing projects or assignments with the major studios. Among those projects is the feature classic, ANTWONE FISHER, directed by and staring Oscar-winning actor Denzel Washington, written by Antwone based on his own life. Antwone received numerous nominations and awards including the renowned Humanitas Prize, Screenwriter of the year award from the National Association Of Theater Owners, and was listed in Variety’s Fifty People to Watch. ”He was also listed among Fade In Magazine’s “100 People in Hollywood you need to know” in 2005.

Antwone’s present screenwriting project is with Columbia Pictures and is presently titled, "the Untitled Antwone Fisher Project”. Antwone made his film-directing debut with the award-winning short film, “My Summer Friend.” He produced wrote and directed the 2013 documentary, This Life of Mine The Incredible Life of Leon Garr.

Elijah Rock

Producer

An Executive Vice President (EVP) of Artistic Services with FanTank Inc., Elijah Rock is merging Arts & Entertainment and internet technology. In content production, he was a founding partner of Decipher Entertainment, a production company that produces film content that uniquely reflects our diverse humanity. He is the winner of the 2014 NAACP Theatre Award and was Nominated for a 2016 Ovation Award. Rock is also a national recording artist of the Great American Songbook.

Brad Wilson

Producer

Legendary Producer, Brad Wilson has worked with Academy Award winning actor Robert Duvall, operating ROBERT DUVALL PRODUCTIONS , producing over eighteen feature films, among them: COLORS with Sean Penn, DAYS OF THUNDER with Tom Cruise and Nicole Kidman, FALLING DOWN with Michael Douglas and the critically acclaimed WRESTLING ERNEST HEMINGWAY with Richard Harris and Shirley MacLaine. Latest films include, MY MANY SONS, based on the life of the most winning basketball coach, Don Meyer, released in 2016 and MEANEST MAN IN TEXAS based on the book, that has completed principal photography and will be released in 2018. This film has been nominated for 26 Film festival Awards and won 16 to date. Wilson's films have also won awards at the Berlin Film Festival, The America Cinema Foundation, The Chicago Film Critics Association, Emden International Film Festival, Nederlands Film Festival, Westchester New York International Film Festival, Stony Brook Film Festival and The Santa Clarita International Film Festival. He is a member of the Academy of Television Arts & Sciences and a charter member of the National Radio Hall of Fame of Texas.
